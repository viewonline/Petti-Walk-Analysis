# Design System Document: The Clinical Sanctuary

This design system is a bespoke framework designed to bridge the gap between rigorous veterinary science and the emotional warmth of pet ownership. It eschews the "standard app" aesthetic in favor of a high-end editorial experience that feels curated, serene, and authoritative.

---

### 1. Overview & Creative North Star
**Creative North Star: "The Clinical Sanctuary"**
The system is built on the philosophy that pet healthcare should feel like a premium wellness retreat rather than a sterile hospital. We achieve this through **Organic Sophistication**: breaking the rigid mobile grid with intentional asymmetry, oversized editorial typography, and "living" layers. 

By utilizing floating elements and overlapping surfaces, we move away from "template" design. The interface should feel like a series of soft, physical cards resting on a clean, light-filled desk.

---

### 2. Colors & Surface Philosophy

Our palette balances the clinical authority of `#00676a` (Primary) with the nurturing warmth of `#8e4e14` (Secondary).

#### The "No-Line" Rule
To maintain a premium, seamless feel, **1px solid borders are strictly prohibited** for sectioning content. Boundaries must be defined through:
*   **Tonal Shifts:** Placing a `surface-container-low` section against the base `surface` background.
*   **Negative Space:** Using the spacing scale to create psychological boundaries.

#### Surface Hierarchy & Nesting
Treat the UI as a series of nested physical layers. 
*   **Base:** `surface` (#f6fafa)
*   **Sections:** `surface-container-low` (#f0f4f4)
*   **Cards:** `surface-container-lowest` (#ffffff) or `surface-container-highest` (#dfe3e3) for emphasized utility.
*   **Interactive Floating Elements:** Use `surface_bright` with a 15% opacity and a 20px backdrop blur to create a "Glassmorphism" effect, allowing the calming background greens to bleed through.

#### Signature Textures
Avoid flat blocks of color for high-impact areas.
*   **CTA Gradients:** For primary buttons or hero sections, use a subtle linear gradient from `primary` (#00676a) to `primary_container` (#298083) at a 135-degree angle. This adds a "soul" to the UI that flat hex codes cannot provide.

---

### 3. Typography: Editorial Authority

We use a dual-font strategy to balance character with legibility.

*   **Display & Headlines (Plus Jakarta Sans):** These are our "Editorial" voices. Use `display-lg` and `headline-md` with tight letter-spacing (-0.02em) to create a sense of professional confidence.
*   **Body & Labels (Inter):** Inter handles the "Functional" voice. It provides high readability for medical data and instructions.

**Typography as Brand:** Use `display-sm` for pet names or health scores, intentionally breaking the grid by overlapping these large text elements slightly over image containers or card edges to create a custom, "magazine-style" layout.

---

### 4. Elevation & Depth: Tonal Layering

Traditional shadows are often "dirty." In this system, we use light and tone to define height.

*   **The Layering Principle:** Instead of a shadow, place a `surface-container-lowest` card on a `surface-container` background. The subtle shift in hex code creates a cleaner, more modern "lift."
*   **Ambient Shadows:** When a float is required (e.g., a Bottom Sheet or Floating Action Button), use an extra-diffused shadow: `Y: 12px, Blur: 24px, Color: #181c1d at 5% opacity`.
*   **The Ghost Border:** For accessibility in form fields, use the `outline-variant` (#bec9c9) at 20% opacity. Never use a 100% opaque border.

---

### 5. Components

#### Buttons (The Soft Touch)
*   **Primary:** Background: `primary` to `primary_container` gradient. Border-radius: `xl` (3rem) for a friendly, pill-shaped feel.
*   **Secondary:** Background: `secondary_fixed`. Text: `on_secondary_fixed`. Use this for pet-centric actions (e.g., "Add Treatment").

#### Cards & Lists (The "No-Divider" Rule)
*   **Forbid the use of divider lines.** Separate health records or appointment lists using `surface-container-low` background blocks or a consistent `1rem` vertical gap.
*   **Status Badges:** Use `tertiary_container` (#5b7984) for neutral statuses and `error_container` (#ffdad6) for urgent medical alerts. Badges must use `full` (9999px) rounding.

#### Specialized Pet Components
*   **The Health Vitals Card:** A `surface-container-lowest` card with an asymmetrical layout—large `display-sm` numbers for weight or heart rate, tucked into the top-right corner, with a `secondary` accent icon in the bottom-left.
*   **Interactive Timeline:** A vertical track using the `outline-variant` at 20% opacity, with `primary` nodes for completed medical events.

---

### 6. Do's and Don'ts

**Do:**
*   **Embrace Whitespace:** If a screen feels "busy," increase the padding to `xl` (3rem) or `lg` (2rem).
*   **Use Asymmetry:** Place a dog profile photo slightly off-center to break the "app-template" feel.
*   **Soft Corners:** Always use the `DEFAULT` (1rem) or `md` (1.5rem) rounding for cards to maintain the "Friendly" vibe.

**Don't:**
*   **Don't use pure black:** Use `on_surface` (#181c1d) for text to keep the contrast high but the "vibe" soft.
*   **Don't use sharp corners:** 0px corners are strictly prohibited as they feel too aggressive for a pet-parenting context.
*   **Don't over-shadow:** If you need more than two layers of shadows on a screen, your tonal layering (background color shifts) has failed. Simplify the surfaces instead.

---

**Director’s Note:** Remember, we are designing for someone who loves their dog but may be stressed about their health. Every interaction should feel like a reassuring, professional hand on their shoulder. Keep it airy, keep it clean, and let the typography do the heavy lifting.**```
